Does Homeopathy Actually Work? What Science Says (With Humor)

Spoiler: it's mostly water. But the story is fascinating.

Let's get this out of the way: homeopathy is one of the most popular alternative therapies on the planet. Millions of people swear by it. Pharmacies sell it. Some doctors recommend it. And yet, when you look at how it works — really look — things get weird. Fast.

The Basics (For the Uninitiated)

Homeopathy was invented in 1796 by Samuel Hahnemann, a German physician. His two core principles:

  • "Like cures like": A substance that causes symptoms in a healthy person can cure those same symptoms in a sick person. Onions make you cry? An onion-based preparation cures your watery-eyed cold.
  • The more you dilute, the stronger it gets: This is where chemistry raises an eyebrow. A typical homeopathic dilution of 30C means the original substance has been diluted 1-in-100, thirty times over. Mathematically, there are zero molecules of the original substance left.

In other words: a homeopathic remedy is, chemically speaking, water. Or sugar, if it's a pellet.

The Water Memory Defense

Homeopaths explain this with the concept of "water memory" — the idea that water "remembers" the substances it has been in contact with and retains their healing essence. It's a poetic idea. It's also not supported by physics or chemistry.

If water had memory, every glass of tap water would be the most powerful medicine in the universe. It's been in contact with everything over its hydrological cycle — dinosaurs, Julius Caesar, a mammoth. Each sip would be a pharmacological cocktail millions of years in the making.

What Does Science Actually Say?

The scientific verdict is clear and consistent:

  • Multiple systematic reviews (Australia's National Health Council, the UK's NHS, the Cochrane Collaboration) all conclude the same thing: homeopathy is not more effective than a placebo.
  • The European Commission stopped funding homeopathic research in 2017 due to lack of results.
  • The UK's NHS dropped homeopathy from its prescribing guidelines.
  • The US FTC requires homeopathic products to state there's no scientific evidence they work.

But Wait — Why Do People Feel Better?

Here's the genuinely interesting part: the homeopathic consultation itself has value. A homeopath spends an hour listening to you — your symptoms, your fears, your dreams, your preferences. In a world where a GP gives you 8 minutes, that hour of undivided attention feels genuinely therapeutic.

The problem isn't that someone listens to you for an hour. The problem is that they then give you sugar water and tell you it's medicine.

The Woo-Woo Meter Verdict

4 out of 5. Science is asking for evidence and nobody has any. The only active molecule in a homeopathic remedy is faith.
